The Ultimate Fluffy Pancakes

Description

Forget the boxed mix; these are the fluffy, diner-style pancakes you’ve been dreaming of. This recipe relies on a simple chemical reaction between the leavening agents and the batter to create those iconic air pockets.

They are thick, soft, and surprisingly easy to whip up with pantry staples. The key is a gentle hand—overmixing the batter is the only way to ruin these, so keep those lumps in there!

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 3 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tbsp white sugar
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 1/4 cups milk
  • 1 egg
  • 3 tbsp butter, melted
  • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, sift together the flour, baking powder, sugar, and salt.
  2. Make a well in the center and pour in the milk, egg, melted butter, and vanilla.
  3. Mix until just combined; it is perfectly fine to have some small lumps in the batter.
  4. Heat a lightly oiled griddle or frying pan over medium-high heat.
  5. Pour or scoop the batter onto the griddle, using approximately 1/4 cup for each pancake.
  6. Wait for bubbles to form on the surface and the edges to look set, then flip and cook until browned on the other side.
  7. Serve warm with butter, syrup, or fresh berries.
